🧘 Exam Mindfulness Tip

If a question looks scary, don't write anything down right away. Take three deep breaths, look at the visual pattern in your head, and remember: math isn't about memorising steps; it's just a puzzle with rules. Use these tools below to see how the puzzles work!

1. Equation Solver & Balancer

Struggling with rearranging or solving? Use this tool to visualize equations as balanced scales. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other!

Play with Balance Scales

2. Interactive Circle Sectors

Change the radius and the sector angle in real-time. Watch how the arc length and area adjust dynamically as the sector grows.

Visualise Sectors

3. Dynamic Venn Diagrams

Unpack intersections, unions, and complements visually. Click different set regions to instantly see their mathematical notations highlighted.

Interact with Sets

4. Interval Notation Builder

Practice writing notations like ]2, 3]. Toggle open and closed endpoints on a slider and see the interval notation update instantly.

5. Desmos: Graph Domain & Range

See exactly how restricting a domain "chops" a graph. Adjust the slider bounds to visualize floor and ceiling range values.

Play with Domain Limits

6. Significant Figures Practice

A quick, pressure-free quiz tool to practice identifying significant figures and rounding numbers without exam stress.
